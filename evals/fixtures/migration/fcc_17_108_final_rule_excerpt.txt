## Section A: Findings on regulatory burden
The Commission has reviewed the record and finds that the prior framework
imposed an unprecedented regulatory power on the internet and risked
smothering innovation across the broadband marketplace. The Commission
concludes that returning to a light-touch framework better balances investment
incentives with consumer protection.

## Section B: Economic impact
Commenters have asserted that the prior approach risked damaging the American
economy and obstructing job creation. The Commission, after reviewing
comments and supporting filings, finds that a lighter regulatory regime is
more likely to encourage capital deployment in broadband infrastructure.

## Section C: Procedural background
This Order responds to comments filed in the docket between 2017 and 2018,
including a substantial volume of bulk and template comments. The Commission
acknowledges the record but bases its decision on the substantive arguments
presented, not on comment volume.
