## 2. Why This Case Matters Historically

The FCC Net Neutrality proceeding `17-108` is historically the **most spammed, coordinated rulemaking proceeding in United States history**. The docket received over **22 million public comments**. 

A subsequent investigation by the New York State Attorney General's Office revealed that **nearly 18 million of those comments were fake**, generated by automated systems and political advocacy groups. Of those, over **8.5 million comments** hijacked the identities of real citizens (often using stolen corporate email databases), and **half a million comments** came from Russian, Chinese, or other foreign domains.

This is the landmark case study demonstrating that **submission volume does not equal democratic voice**. It represents the definitive problem Astroturf was designed to solve.

---

## 3. Known Astroturf Campaigns Involved

The primary coordinated effort captured in our 5K demo window is the **Broadband for America (BFA)** campaign:
- **Funding & Sponsor**: Funded by a coalition of major telecommunications trade groups (including NCTA, CTIA, and USTelecom).
- **Stated Position**: Opposed the 2015 Title II Net Neutrality rules (Obama-era regulations) and supported the FCC's proposed deregulation under Chairman Ajit Pai.
- **Methodology**: Used commercial lead-generation firms to submit over **1.5 million comments** in a highly automated, bulk copy-paste fashion. Many of these submissions used stolen names without the individuals' consent.

## 4. Expected Benchmark Phrases

The BFA campaign utilized several slightly varied templates. The most dominant template contains the following **canonical benchmark phrase**:

> "The unprecedented regulatory power the Obama Administration imposed on the internet is smothering innovation, damaging the American economy and obstructing job creation."

## 5. Local Slice Limitations & Analysis Scope

- **Temporal Scoping**: Our local validation run uses comments submitted between **August 28, 2017** and **August 30, 2017**. Coordinated spikes are narrow; this window catches a significant BFA filing burst.
- **Coverage**: By focusing on the direct inline comment body (rather than attached PDFs), the clustering pipeline successfully groups text submissions using `BAAI/bge-large-en-v1.5` embeddings.
- **Floor Verification**: The exact-hash baseline is run alongside embedding connected-components clustering to show that while literal duplicate group counts remain small, embedding clustering aggregates close paraphrases into a massive single campaign cluster.
